My Favorite Fishes

Betta Fish

Betta fish, also known as Siamese fighting fish, are small, colorful freshwater fish native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Vietnam. Known for their vibrant hues and long, flowing fins, they are popular pets, especially males, who display striking colors and elaborate fin shapes. Bettas are territorial and often aggressive toward other males, so they should be kept alone or with peaceful species. They thrive in warm water, requiring a clean tank of at least 5 gallons, and are carnivorous, feeding on protein-rich foods like pellets, brine shrimp, and bloodworms. With proper care, bettas can live 3 to 5 years, making them relatively low-maintenance but visually captivating companions.

Guppie Fish

Guppies, also known as Poecilia reticulata, are small, colorful freshwater fish native to South America. They're popular for their vibrant colors, active behavior, and ease of care, making them ideal for beginner aquarists. Guppies come in a wide range of colors and patterns, with males often more brightly colored than females. They're livebearers, meaning they give birth to free-swimming fry instead of laying eggs. Guppies are peaceful, social fish that thrive in groups and can coexist with many other species. They prefer a warm tank with clean water and a diet of high-quality flakes, frozen, or live food. With proper care, guppies can live for about 2 to 3 years.
